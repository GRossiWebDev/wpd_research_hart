White User Persona

Name: Becky Harrington

Demographic: Female 25-35 years of age

Experiences:

  • Becky has visited websites that have been about recipes or cooking help.
  • When on the site she stated that she looks for ingredient quality (which depends on the item she is looking up to cook.)
  • Does not go to specific recipes.
  • She goes to the "homemade" recipe sites.
  • She stated that she has been to multiple sites where the recipe was hidden under a bunch of different content or ads and this made her look to a different site.
  • In her experiences Becky has been discouraged if a recipe appears too difficult and browsed other websites until she either found an easier recipe or a different recipe.
  • Becky has never went online to ask a food/cooking related question.
  • Becky has never visited a site to figure out cooking problems, albeit interested to see how it would work, she only ever visited these type of sites for recipes.

Preferences:

  • She does not like it when websites try to get fancy with a bunch of "foodtalk" in their recipes, she prefers it to be the recipe.
  • She likes when the recipe includes a picture of the finished product so she can see how bad she is failing at the recipe.
  • Becky prefers when the layout of the websites are simple. "I am here for the recipe and ingredients, I want to get an idea of what I am going to get."
  • Depending on what is looked up, Becky likes to see alternate ingredients to the recipes. (for example, if you look at pancake batter, all the ingredients are on the box but it says you can also use water instead of buttermilk).
  • Becky likes when the recipes in the websites offer substitutions. "You are already stressed out enough trying to find a recipe."
  • "I am not a video person. I would rather have a step by step instruction, I am not going to watch and then skim through a whole video."
  • Some websites have both which Becky does not mind since the video can be skipped.
  • Becky likes a summary of the recipe on top of the page. She showed a page which listed the:
    • Level of difficulty
    • Yield (Servings)
    • Cook Time
    • Prep Time
  • Becky goes on ingredients rather than reviews when picking a recipe.
  • Becky stated some recipes offer sides that would be good with recipes which is "pretty cool" so then she knows what goes good with the meal.
  • Becky suggested a leftovers recipe section at the bottom of the recipe which would "be cool", so she knows what she can do with her remaining meal after.

Clutter Competitive Analysis

Food52 is a blog site for recipes, living, and travel, and tries to push the users to buy items they are selling. The website does have a Q&A area. It is called Hotline.

The site seems lacking in colors, as white and grey are the main two colors used through the site, with little bits of gold used. The gold is used on links and buttons that are not main navigation. There is gold also on a blog's rating.

It isn't lacking in pictures except in the Q&A area. The blogs and home page has pictures everywhere while the Q&A area is only the questions.

The Q&A area is able to be split into urgent, unanswered, and topics. The questions can be answered by anyone. The answers are voted on by others on the site and brings the top most 'liked' comment to the top.

Rossi Competitive Analysis

GimmeSomeOven is a culinary blog site that showcases how to tutorials, videos, and printable recipes. While the website shows videos if you click on a specific recipe, they also display videos on their YouTube account that is linked to the website.

The website by itself has little to no color. However, the recipes bring fun and color to an otherwise black and white page. It reminds me of looking at a fancy personal cookbook.

The website displays excellently on a mobile device. However, space is not utilized well on larger devices and screens. The website does not increase the size of text as the screen size gets larger.

Each recipe has between five and seven images that showcase what the dish is supposed to look like. This helps as some recipes people may not know. They understand this and give a brief history on a dish that potential chefs may have pushed away because they did not understand what they were actually making. An example of this can be seen here:. Cacio-e-pepe

On every recipe post, there is an option to print out the recipe or pin it on Pinterest. It also includes materials and equipment that you can buy on Amazon.
